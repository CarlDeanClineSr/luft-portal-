## Law #10: Polarity-Gated Preload (Restitutive Law)

**χ climbs under south Bz, unloads under north**

The vacuum modulation amplitude χ responds asymmetrically to interplanetary magnetic field (IMF) orientation: it increases (preloads) under southward Bz and decreases (unloads) under northward Bz—a restitutive behavior showing the vacuum has polarity memory.

## Polarity Response

**Southward Bz (negative):**
- Magnetic field opposes Earth's field

**Northward Bz (positive):**

## Restitutive Mechanism

The vacuum acts like a **polarity-gated spring**:

1. **Preload Phase (South Bz):**
   - Magnetopause reconnection injects energy
   - Vacuum lattice compresses (χ rises)
   - Energy stored as lattice strain

2. **Locked Phase:**
   - Energy held in metastable configuration
   - χ maintains elevated plateau (ratchet)
   - Minimal relaxation

3. **Unload Phase (North Bz):**
   - Reconnection weakens
   - Vacuum lattice expands (χ falls)
   - Energy released slowly

The asymmetry (faster preload, slower unload) creates **hysteresis**—the vacuum remembers its loading history.
